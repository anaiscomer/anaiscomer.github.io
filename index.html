In the depths of winter, the sea ice is at its thickest and he prowls into the middle of the ocean. He takes his prey from underneath his feet. \n\nHe feeds on the ringed seal, swimming underneath metres of ice. This seal twists and turns in the ocean, and can hold its breath for forty minutes. But eventually he will need to come to the surface for air, this is where he meets the polar bear. \n\nHe builds a network of breathing holes through the ice. And the polar bear chooses one of these holes and waits there for days. The polar bear is a patient and noble hunter. \n\nOnly a handful of animals can survive in this climate, and the polar bear, our lonely huntsman, rules this kingdom. \n\nThis kingdom of ice and stillness, of darkness and moonlight. A cold white world. \n\nAnd it is in February, the day after Valentines that our friend the sun discovers this seductive kingdom. \n\nAt first he takes tentative steps.\n\nHe is shy, he only rises for an hour at a time. \n\nBut he is drawn in by the beauty of the tundra, its noble creatures, the vast glaciers that flow from lonely peaks. \n\nAnd so he stays longer each time, as he grows in confidence. And this growth brings small creatures from the darkest depths of the ocean and feed from the suns energy, bottling the artic sunshine in their bellies.\n\nAnd as our friend intensifies his courtship, his passion and his heat melt that artic bridge of ice, opening the door to Svalbard’s rich waters. \n\nSlowly our friend breaks the silence of winter. And in his wake come new companions, the distant calls of seabirds sound the first inhabitants. With each call more and more swarm.\n\nHe is in the height of his passions, he bathes Svalbard in perpetual light. It becomes the land of the midnight sun, he is intoxicated. \nHe circles the horizon, caught in an intensity of passion, and his warmth begins to transform the landscape. As his lust reaches further, the whole tundra starts to bloom. \n\nHis companions marvel at his passions and feed on his riches. He has revealed a green world under this layer of white.\n\nHis presence sometimes shocks them. His passion is sometimes too much. So that occasionally they break from him taking rest and shelter on those small islands of snow that have yet to succumb to his touch.\n\nBy July his passions have reached their most destructive level. Warm outbursts are destroying the iciness that drew him in. He brings out the frenzy in this still land, his energy sends glaciers into movement. \n\nNo longer is it a place of solitude, of quiet stillness. Svalbard is now a traffic of life, loud and demanding. \n\nAnd that first creature is struggling. The polar bear is losing a grip on his kingdom.\n\nOur friend watches as his first companion loses his regal power. No longer the noble and patient hunter, the bear is now a scavenger. Preying on anything he can find, the weak the young. And being thwarted by even the smallest of creatures.\n\nAll that our friend was seduced by, in those first few tentative hours, has gone, the noble silent land replaced by one that saps more and more of our friend’s energy. \n\nIn his presence he has brought an intense and demanding wealth of life. Our sun grows tired; his passions are waning. And so he gradually drifts apart from Svalbard.\n\nAnd when our friend’s energy had touched even the stillest corners of the islands.\n\nWhen the ice cliffs of Austfonna begin to melt, when even the most impenetrable blocks of ice start to flow, then our friend hears his cue. \n\nHis passions quickly muting.\nHe withdraws from this place.\n\nHe has changed what he loved about this icy kingdom, with just his presence. And as he withdraws a little more each day so do his companions. \n\nAnd the icy kingdom grows quieter each day.\n\nAnd as he drops below the horizon for the last time, he catches a glimpse of the polar bear. Noble and regal once again, the polar bear has regained control of his empire. And as Svalbard returns to the dark stillness of winter, our friend, the sun, sees again that stillness that seduced him in the first place.
